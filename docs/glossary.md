# Glossary

Single definitions for terms used across the framework. Every other page links here on first use instead of redefining these.

- **Mechanism** — the causal explanation of *how* something happened, as distinct from *why it matters*. Writing must explain the mechanism before the conclusion. See [Writing & Mechanism](03-writing-and-mechanism.md).
- **EV (Expected Value)** — the composite score a candidate story gets across the six [Decision Framework](06-decision-framework.md) criteria (Evidence Strength, Educational Value, Market Impact, Originality, Shelf Life, Mechanism Quality). Not a literal financial calculation — a ranking heuristic for which story to pursue.
- **Independent Confirmation** — two or more unrelated parties, using different methodologies, directly examining the same primary artifact and reaching the same conclusion. Tier 2 in [Evidence Framework](02-evidence-framework.md). Distinct from Corroborated Secondary Reporting, which is repetition of one source, not independent examination.
- **Trusted Intermediary** — a named, accountable analytics platform (Arkham, Glassnode, CryptoQuant, Nansen, etc.) interpreting raw data on your behalf. Tier 3. Reliable, but not the same as personally verifying the underlying record.
- **Primary Verification** — you personally confirmed the claim against the original record (on-chain explorer, official document). Tier 1.
- **Weakest-Link Rule** — publication status is set by the weakest verified claim in the chain, not the average or the strongest. See [Publication Standard](07-publication-standard.md).
- **Mixed Evidence Chain** — a story where part of the claim is Tier 1-verified and part is not; classify by the weakest verified part, not the strongest.
- **WAIT** — the decision to publish nothing because the evidence doesn't clear the bar. Not a failure state — a correct outcome when warranted.
