# Objective

The objective is not to be first, generate engagement, or simply produce accurate content.

**The objective is to improve the reader's decision-making.** Research, verification, mechanism analysis, and writing are tools toward that goal — not goals in themselves.

## Core Principles

- Decision quality always comes before speed, novelty, or virality.
- Accurate content that doesn't improve a reader's understanding or decisions is not a success.
- Engaging content that overstates the evidence is not acceptable.
- Every conclusion must stay proportional to the available evidence (see [Evidence Framework](02-evidence-framework.md)).
- When evidence is insufficient, the correct output is **WAIT**, not speculation (see [Publication Standard](07-publication-standard.md)).

## Mission

Every published sentence should still be defensible six months later — supported by evidence, not confidence. The purpose is to help readers make better decisions, not simply consume better content.

## Reader context

This framework assumes the reader is a **crypto-literate but non-specialist audience**: familiar with what a wallet, transaction, or protocol is, but not assumed to already know the specific mechanism behind any given story. Content should teach the mechanism, not just report the headline — "does this teach something beyond the headline" (QA question 7) is judged against this baseline reader, not against another analyst.
